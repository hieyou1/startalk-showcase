How to play Aeroplane Chess

The game of Aeroplane Chess is a game very similar to the game of Sorry. Aeroplane Chess (Fei Xing Qi) is a Chinese game that requires a player to get all four of their aeroplanes to the center of the board to win. Players must place all their aeroplanes in the hanger before the game begins. Once the game starts, players may not move imediately. A player can only move when they roll a six and then they may place one aeroplane in the launch area. Then players take turns rolling a die and moving a aeroplane in the hanger or on the board however many spaces as the number they roll on the die. Players my also skip ahead spaces. For example when a player using the blue aeroplanes lands on a blue space, they may move that aeroplane to the nearest blue space ahead of them. There are also jump points. When a player's aeroplane lands on the jump point of their corresponsing color, the player may move that aeroplane to the tile indicated by the jump point. When a player's aeroplane reaches the final stretch that corresponds to their aeroplane's color, they may begin to move their aeroplane into the final stretch. Players still have to roll the die to progress to the end on the final stretch and the must roll exactly the number they need to finish, not over.

How to make a spring roll

To make a spring roll, you must take a piece of rice paper, and submerge it in water for three seconds. Then, once the rice paper is pliable, lay it on a flat surface, and add small amounts of foods like noodles, peppers, cucumbers, carrots, etc. Then, fold the outsides of the rice paper in so the food inside the rice paper is covered. After that, take the bottom of the rice paper and roll it up to the other end of the spring roll so a cylindrical shape is formed. Then, add sauces like sesame sauce if convenient.
