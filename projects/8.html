Journal to the West

  • Journey to the West is regarded as one of the greatest Classic Chinese Novels
  • It has also been described as arguably the most popular literary work in East Asia.
  • Written by Wu Cheng’en, a novelist and poet of the Ming dynasty (1368–1644).
  • 100 chapters, which can be divided into 3 main parts
  • The first 7 chapters deal with the birth of a monkey, Sun Wukong, from a stone egg and his acquisition of magic powers.
  • The next 5 chapters describe the story of a monk named Tang Sanzang, and the origin of his mission to the West.
  • The bulk of the novel recounts the 81 adventures that befall Tang Sanzang and his entourage of three animal spirits on their journey to India, in search of Buddhist Sutras
